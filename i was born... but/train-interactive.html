This worker represents how the urban space has colonized the suburbs.
The fast speed of the train highlights its intrusive nature, which contrasts with the calmness of the suburbs. This emphasizes the difference between these two distinct worlds.
The train itself connects the urban and suburban spaces together. The suburbs' reliance on the urban schedule reveals the dependent nature of the suburbs on the city, highlighting how the city has "colonized" the suburbs.
The "world" on the other side of the tracks is a trap. Although far from the suburbs in terms of distance, the people who live in the suburbs must rely on the city, therefore being trapped by it.
